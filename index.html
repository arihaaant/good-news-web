Why IVF

Discover the top reasons why IVF has become a leading fertility treatment option for many couples.

IVF Treatment

IVF Treatment, or In Vitro Fertilization, offers hope to couples facing infertility by facilitating the fertilization of eggs with sperm outside the body and transferring resulting embryos to the uterus.

Female Infertility

Female infertility refers to the inability of a woman to conceive or carry a pregnancy to term. It can result from various factors, such as hormonal imbalances or structural issues in the reproductive system. In vitro fertilization (IVF) is a common assisted reproductive technique that can help overcome female infertility by combining eggs and sperm outside the body, increasing the chances of successful conception.

Cryopreservation

Cryopreservation preserves reproductive materials like eggs and embryos through freezing, ensuring the possibility of future IVF treatments, even after an initial cycle.

IVF Egg Donation

IVF Egg Donation involves the use of donated eggs in the IVF process, granting hope to individuals or couples who require the assistance of a generous egg donor to achieve their dream of parenthood.

Blood Treatment

Blood Treatment encompasses various therapies involving blood products, which can aid in improving fertility-related medical conditions or enhancing overall health during the IVF journey.

Male Infertility

Male Infertility treatments address reproductive issues in men, ensuring that they have the best chances of fathering a child and contributing to a successful IVF journey for couples.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to commonly asked questions about IVF and its process.

  • What is IVF and how does it work?

    IVF, or In Vitro Fertilization, is a fertility treatment where eggs and sperm are combined in a laboratory dish, and resulting embryos are transferred to the uterus to establish a pregnancy.

  • Who is a suitable candidate for IVF?

    IVF can be a suitable option for couples dealing with infertility due to various factors, including fallopian tube damage, ovulation disorders, endometriosis, male factor infertility, and unexplained infertility.

  • What is the success rate of IVF?

    The success rate of IVF can vary depending on various factors, including the age of the woman, the underlying cause of infertility, and the quality of embryos transferred. Your fertility specialist can provide personalized information based on your specific circumstances.

  • What is ICSI, and when is it used in IVF?

    ICSI, or Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection, is a technique used during IVF where a single sperm is directly injected into an egg to enhance fertilization in cases of male factor infertility, such as low sperm count or poor sperm motility.

  • What is genetic testing in IVF, and why is it done?

    Genetic testing, also known as preimplantation genetic testing (PGT), is performed during IVF to screen embryos for genetic abnormalities or chromosomal disorders. It helps select the healthiest embryos for transfer and can reduce the risk of certain genetic conditions in offspring.

  • Are there any risks associated with IVF?

    While IVF is generally a safe procedure, it may carry some risks, including ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS), multiple pregnancies, ectopic pregnancy, and a slightly increased risk of birth defects. Your fertility specialist will discuss these risks and guide you through the process.
